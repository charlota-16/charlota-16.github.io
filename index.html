Understanding Growth and Decay

Growth and decay describe how quantities increase or decrease over time. These concepts are used in fields like biology, economics, and physics to model real-world phenomena such as population growth, radioactive decay, or even the growth of investments.

General Formula:
P(t) = P₀ * e^(kt)
  • P(t): The quantity at time t
  • P₀: The initial quantity
  • k: Growth rate (k > 0) or decay rate (k < 0)
  • t: Time
Example: A bacteria culture starts with 100 bacteria and doubles every 2 hours. How many bacteria will there be after 6 hours?
  1. Using P(t) = P₀ * e^(kt)
  2. Where:
    • P₀ = 100
    • k = ln(2)/2
    • t = 6
  3. Result: 800 bacteria

Newton's Law of Cooling

Newton's Law of Cooling states that the rate at which an object cools is proportional to the difference between its temperature and the ambient temperature.

Formula:
T(t) = Tₐ + (T₀ - Tₐ) * e^(-kt)
  • T(t): Temperature at time t
  • Tₐ: Ambient temperature
  • T₀: Initial temperature
  • k: Cooling constant
Example: A cup of coffee cools from 90°C to 60°C in 10 minutes in a room with an ambient temperature of 20°C. What will the coffee's temperature be after 20 minutes?

Step-by-step Calculation:
  1. Using the formula: T(t) = Tₐ + (T₀ - Tₐ) * e^(-kt)
  2. Here:
    • Tₐ = 20 (ambient temperature)
    • T₀ = 90 (initial temperature)
    • Find k using the first 10 minutes: 60 = 20 + (90 - 20) * e^(-10k)
  3. Solve for k:
    • 40 = 70 * e^(-10k)
    • e^(-10k) = 40 / 70 ≈ 0.571
    • -10k = ln(0.571) ≈ -0.56
    • k ≈ 0.056
  4. After 20 minutes:
    • T(20) = 20 + (90 - 20) * e^(-0.056 * 20)
    • e^(-1.12) ≈ 0.326
    • T(20) = 20 + 70 * 0.326 ≈ 42.8°C

Answer: The coffee's temperature after 20 minutes will be approximately 42.8°C.
